Moon Phases

New Moon

The moon is not visible from Earth during the New Moon phase. The side facing Earth is not illuminated.

Waxing Crescent

A small, crescent-shaped portion of the moon is visible. The illuminated portion is increasing, leading up to the First Quarter.

First Quarter

Half of the moon is illuminated, and it appears as a half-moon shape. This phase marks the first visible quarter of the lunar cycle.

Waxing Gibbous

The illuminated portion of the moon is more than half, but not yet a full moon. It continues to grow larger, leading up to the Full Moon.

Full Moon

The entire face of the moon is illuminated and visible from Earth. It is the midpoint of the lunar cycle and appears as a complete circle.

Waning Gibbous

The illuminated portion is more than half, but decreasing. It follows the Full Moon and leads to the Last Quarter.

Last Quarter

Half of the moon is illuminated, and it appears as a half-moon shape. This phase marks the last visible quarter of the lunar cycle.

Waning Crescent

A small, crescent-shaped portion of the moon is visible. The illuminated portion is decreasing, leading back to the New Moon.
